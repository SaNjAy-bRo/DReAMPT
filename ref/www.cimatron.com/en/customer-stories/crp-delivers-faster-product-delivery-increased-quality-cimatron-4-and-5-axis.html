Home > Resource Center > Case Studies > CRP Delivers Faster Product Delivery, Increased Quality With Cimatron 4- and 5-axis Machining

Motorsport company CRP Technology has been using Cimatron's CAD/CAM solutions, particularly the 5-Axis solution, to produce high-quality parts with faster production time and the ability to make design changes easily.

CRP Delivers Faster Product Delivery, Increased Quality With Cimatron 4- and 5-axis Machining

Industry

Motorsport

Location

Modena, Italy

Website

http://www.crptechnology.com

Challenge
  • Producing parts to perfection in line with customers’ needs
  • Achieving on-time delivery, even if production of a part involves a high degree of complexity
  • Handling the design changes to the product that are inevitable during motor racing seasons
Solutions

Cimatron’s 4- and 5-Axis

Results
  • Faster production resulting from system automation and overall integration efficiencies
  • Improved product quality owing to powerful automation which reduces the chance of human errors, and the ability to simulate realistically the process of chip removal
  • The capacity to make changes to the product design relatively easily, even once NC programming has begun

CRP owes its origins to the creation of tool shop Roberto Cevolini & Co in 1970, which produced components for Formula 1 vehicles, including frame parts, gearboxes, engine components and machined wind tunnel parts. CRP Technology was established in 1996 as the research and development arm of the business, later merging with the parent business under the CRP banner. CRP’s design and production of components for racing cars and two-wheelers have played an invaluable role in the success of many F1 teams, Rally Raid, ALMS, and World Rally.

CRP has used Cimatron’s CAD/CAM solutions since the 1980s. Of particular importance has been Cimatron’s 5-Axis solution, which CRP uses in the processing of materials such as titanium alloys, metal, and special steels. Some of Cimatron’s 5-Axis functionalities used by CRP have been constant collision control between tools, shanks, and holders; roughing out in spiral, parallel, isoparametric, pencil and morph motions between contours and surfaces; rapid machining of deep cavities; and the milling of profiles.
